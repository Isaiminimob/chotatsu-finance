How the Interest Calculator Works

An interest calculator is a fundamental financial tool that helps you understand how your money can grow over time through investments, or how much extra you'll pay on a loan. It can calculate both simple and compound interest, two key concepts in personal finance.

Simple Interest is calculated only on the principal amount of a loan or deposit. It does not take into account any interest that has accumulated in previous periods. It's a straightforward calculation and is often used for short-term loans.

The formula for simple interest is: $$ \text{Simple Interest} = \frac{P \times R \times T}{100} $$ Where: - $P$ = Principal Amount - $R$ = Annual Interest Rate - $T$ = Time Period in Years

Compound Interest, on the other hand, is calculated on the principal amount and also on the accumulated interest from previous periods. This is often referred to as 'interest on interest' and can lead to significant growth over long periods. This is the magic behind long-term investments.

The formula for compound interest (compounded annually) is: $$ \text{Total Amount} = P(1 + \frac{R}{100})^T $$ $$ \text{Compound Interest} = \text{Total Amount} - P $$

By using this calculator, you can compare the outcomes of simple versus compound interest and make more informed decisions about your savings, investments, or loans.
